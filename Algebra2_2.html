2.4: Writing Equations of Lines

Writing an Equation of a Line

Slope-Intercept Form

Given the slope \(m\) and the y-intercept \(b\), use this equation: \(y=mx+b\)

Point-Slope Form

Given the slope \(m\) and a point \((x, y)\), Use this equation: \(y-y_1=m(x-x_1)\)

Two Points

Given two points \((x_1, y_1)\) and \((x_2, y_2)\), use the formula: \(m=\frac{y_2-y_1}{x_2-x_1}\) to find the slope \(m\). Then use the Point-Slope form with this slope and either of the given points to write an equation.
